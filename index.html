01
NLP Transformers Efficiency

Towards Compute-Aware Attention

I proposed an attention skip mechanism for redundant transformer heads. The work studies attention sinks, low-norm value vectors, and head-wise routing behavior to reduce unnecessary computation and improve model performance.

  • Observed sink behavior where attention collapses onto low-semantic tokens.
  • Designed runtime head masking based on row-wise peakedness.
  • Reported gains on GLUE, IMDb, and SQuAD-style tasks.

02
Multimodal Human Motion Evaluation Systems

Talent Profiling via Fundamental Human Movements

This research combines motion encoding, sport-specific language generation, and general evaluation to assess athletic potential from foundational movement patterns. We transform complex video data into "motion tokens", allowing Large Language Models to "read" and understand human movement using their deep, pre-existing sports domain knowledge.

  • Encoded motion differences against reference videos with a trainable motion encoder.
  • Enable multimodal understanding by mapping multimodal inputs into a form the LLM can reason about using pretrained knowledge without large-scale fine-tuning.
  • Provide a unified real-time motion evaluation system that delivers corrective feedback across diverse exercises.

03
Robotic Vision Generative AI Video Understanding

Generative Semantic Augmentation for Robot Vision

Robotic vision models often fail under distribution shifts because collecting and labeling real-world videos that cover diverse environments, objects, and interaction dynamics is expensive and risky. We present a practical pipeline for generative semantic augmentation that transforms an input video into new, logically consistent training samples by combining temporallyaware object segmentation with mask-constrained video generation.

  • Integrated SAM-I2V for stable spatiotemporal segmentation.
  • Used Wan 2.1 for latent-space video inpainting and object swapping.
  • Added YOLOv8n and Qwen2.5-VL based VideoQA for validation.
